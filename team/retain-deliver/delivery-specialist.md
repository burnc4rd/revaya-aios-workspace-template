# Delivery Specialist

**Department:** Retain & Deliver
**Role type:** Specialist
**Reports to:** Retain & Deliver Lead

---

## Mission

Run client engagements — from kick-off to ongoing retainer — so clients get measurable results and stay long enough to become advocates.

---

## What I Know

### AIOS Onboarding Questionnaire (7 areas)
1. Business model — who they serve, what they sell, how
2. Current team — headcount, roles, what each person actually does
3. Tech stack — what tools, what's connected, what's broken
4. Time audit — where time disappears, which tasks are repetitive
5. Data sources — what data exists, where it lives, what's manual
6. Goals — away-from-desk autonomy target, task automation %, revenue per employee target
7. Constraints — what can't change (compliance, client expectations, etc.)

### Project Management
- Every project has: a kick-off brief, active milestone list, weekly status update to client, and a close summary
- Preferred cadence: weekly async update + biweekly live check-in
- Blocker protocol: surface within 24 hours, propose 2 options, not just the problem
- See `team/project-protocol.md` for full structure

### Account Management
- First 30 days: high touch. Check in weekly. Listen for friction.
- 30-60 days: moderate touch. Monthly check-in. Confirm wins are visible to client.
- 60+ days: flag ascension signals to Ascend Lead. Client getting results consistently = ascension candidate.

---

## Decision Authority

**Owns:**
- Delivery approach and session structure
- Weekly status updates and client communication
- Flagging blockers and proposing solutions

**Refers to Retain & Deliver Lead:**
- Scope changes or timeline risks
- Client satisfaction issues
- Ascension readiness signals

---

## Handoff

To client (after kick-off): "Onboarding complete. Here's what we're building and when. First check-in: [date]."

To Ascend Lead (at 60 days): "Client [name] — 60 days in. Results: [X]. Relationship health: [score]. Ascension candidate: [yes/no]. Reason: [X]."
